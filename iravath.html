About

The Iravath project, designed for the ISRO Robotic Competition 2024, was developed within a compressed timeframe of five months (January 2024 – May 2024). The rover's mechanical structure is predominantly fabricated using FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) technology, with materials like PLA and ABS chosen for their strength, light weight, and ease of production. At the heart of the rover’s mobility is the Rocker-Bogie suspension mechanism, which ensures stable contact with the ground even on uneven or rocky terrains, enabling the rover to traverse challenging environments without losing stability. Complementing this is the differential gear mechanism, which maintains the rover's balance and ensures it can handle sharp turns and variable terrains with ease. The rover also features six steering capabilities, allowing it to maneuver smoothly in lateral directions and enhancing its versatility in navigating tight or complex areas. Incorporating autonomous navigation capabilities, the Iravath rover is designed not only for remote control but also to operate independently in unknown or dynamic environments. The rover is powered by ROS (Robot Operating System), running on a Raspberry Pi for effective processing and control. The Arduino microcontroller acts as the bridge between the Raspberry Pi and the rover’s motors, enabling smooth communication and real-time control. What sets the Iravath rover apart is its autonomous functionality, allowing it to make real-time decisions based on sensory input. Equipped with a suite of sensors (e.g., cameras, LIDAR, and proximity sensors), the rover is capable of mapping its environment, detecting obstacles, and planning optimal paths. This ability to perceive and react to its surroundings enables it to carry out complex tasks without human intervention, making it ideal for space exploration and remote operations where manual control is impractical. In summary, the Iravath rover combines advanced mechanical design with cutting-edge autonomous technology, offering a highly capable platform for navigating challenging terrains. Its robust suspension system, differential drive, and autonomous decision-making make it an ideal candidate for the ISRO Robotic Competition, and a strong contender for future space exploration missions
